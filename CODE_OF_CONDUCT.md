# StadiaIQ Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We, the contributors and maintainers of StadiaIQ, pledge to make participation
in our project and community a harassment-free experience for everyone,
regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex
characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education,
socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color,
religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming,
diverse, inclusive, and healthy community — both within this repository and in
any venue where StadiaIQ is discussed or demonstrated.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language in issues, PRs, and code comments
- Providing constructive, actionable feedback during code reviews
- Respecting differing viewpoints on architectural decisions (Express middleware
  ordering, React component patterns, Zod schema design, etc.)
- Gracefully accepting and offering critique
- Focusing on what benefits StadiaIQ's users — fans, stadium staff, and
  administrators alike
- Showing empathy toward fellow contributors, especially newcomers

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- Trolling, insulting, or derogatory comments in any project channel
- Personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- Sustained disruption of discussions or deliberate derailing of technical
  threads
- Any conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a
  professional setting

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all StadiaIQ project spaces — including the
GitHub repository, issue tracker, pull request discussions, project
documentation, and any community chat channels. It also applies when an
individual is officially representing the project in public spaces such as
conferences, meetups, or social media.

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing the standards of
acceptable behavior. They have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or
reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that
do not align with this Code of Conduct. Maintainers will communicate reasons for
moderation decisions when appropriate.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, please
report it by emailing:

**conduct@stadiaiq.dev**

All reports will be reviewed promptly and handled confidentially. The project
team is committed to maintaining the privacy and security of the reporter.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Project maintainers will follow these guidelines when determining consequences
for violations:

### 1. Correction

**Trigger:** Minor, first-time infractions such as unintentionally unwelcoming
language.

**Action:** A private written warning explaining the violation and why the
behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

### 2. Warning

**Trigger:** A repeated minor infraction or a single more significant violation.

**Action:** A formal warning with specified consequences for continued behavior.
The individual may be asked to avoid interaction with affected parties for a
defined period. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary ban.

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Trigger:** A serious violation or sustained pattern of inappropriate behavior.

**Action:** A temporary ban from all project interaction — issues, PRs, code
review, and community channels — for a specified period. No public or private
contact with the people involved is permitted during the ban.

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Trigger:** A pattern of violations, harassment, or aggression directed at
individuals or groups.

**Action:** Permanent removal from all project spaces with no opportunity for
reinstatement.
